# Problem Statement Document
**Let's Tour Zimbabwe Platform**

## Executive Summary
The educational tourism sector in Zimbabwe faces significant transparency, pricing, and service delivery challenges that negatively impact schools' ability to provide quality educational experiences for their students.

## Problem Definition

### Core Problem
Schools in Zimbabwe struggle with opaque, inefficient, and often exploitative processes when organizing educational tours through registered touring companies, leading to:
- Overcharging without transparent pricing breakdown
- Under-delivery on promised services and itineraries  
- Potential corruption through teacher bribes influencing company selection
- Lack of standardized quality assurance for service providers

### Problem Scope
- **Geographic**: Zimbabwe (initial focus: Kadoma region)
- **Sector**: Educational tourism for schools
- **Stakeholders Affected**: 
  - ~10,000 schools (**REQUIRES VALIDATION**: estimated 2,500 conduct annual tours)
  - ~100+ registered touring companies (**REQUIRES VALIDATION**: actual number unknown)
  - Thousands of students missing quality educational experiences
  - Service providers (chefs, guides, transport, accommodation) lacking fair market access

### Current State Challenges

#### For Schools:
1. **Lack of Transparency**: No standardized pricing or service comparison tools
2. **Limited Options**: Restricted to known touring companies, often through informal networks
3. **Quality Uncertainty**: No reliable rating system for service providers
4. **Administrative Burden**: Complex manual processes for tour planning and approval
5. **Budget Constraints**: Difficulty optimizing costs across multiple service components

#### For Service Providers:
1. **Market Access**: Limited visibility to potential school clients
2. **Unfair Competition**: Established companies dominate through relationships rather than merit
3. **Pricing Pressure**: No standardized pricing framework
4. **Quality Standards**: Lack of certification or rating systems

#### For Students:
1. **Subpar Experiences**: Poor service delivery due to cost-cutting by touring companies
2. **Limited Destinations**: Restricted options based on touring company capabilities
3. **Educational Value**: Tours may not align with educational objectives

## Problem Impact

### Quantitative Impact (**REQUIRES VALIDATION**)
- **Market Size**: Approximately 2,500 schools conducting tours annually (**UNVALIDATED ESTIMATE**)
- **Financial Impact**: Estimated overpayment of 15-30% due to lack of price transparency (**REQUIRES SURVEY DATA**)
- **Service Delivery**: Estimated 40% of tours fail to meet promised itinerary standards (**REQUIRES VALIDATION STUDY**)

### Qualitative Impact
- Erosion of trust between schools and touring industry
- Reduced educational value from poorly planned tours
- Missed opportunities for local service providers
- Potential reputational damage to Zimbabwe's tourism sector

## Root Causes
1. **Information Asymmetry**: Schools lack access to comprehensive service provider information
2. **Market Fragmentation**: No centralized platform connecting schools with service providers
3. **Regulatory Gaps**: Limited oversight of touring company service delivery standards
4. **Process Inefficiency**: Manual, paper-based planning and booking processes

## Success Criteria for Solution
A successful solution must:
- Increase pricing transparency by 100%
- Reduce tour planning time by 60%
- Improve service delivery satisfaction scores by 50%
- Provide equal market access for all certified service providers
- Ensure compliance with ZTA and Ministry of Education requirements
